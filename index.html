I am a tenure-track assistant professor in the Department of Computational Mathematics, Science & Engineering (CMSE) at Michigan State University. I received my Ph.D. in Statistics at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA), where I was advised by Dr. Jingyi Jessica Li. I hold an M.S. in Statistics from Zhejiang University, where I worked with Dr. Yi Zhang, and dual bachelor's degrees in Mathematics and Economics from Shandong University.

My research focuses on developing statistical methods to address challenges in real-world data analysis. My areas of interest include:

  1. Statistical Bioinformatics

    1. Statistical methods for analyzing high-dimensional single-cell and spatial omics data

    2. Using synthetic data to enhance the statistical rigor in single-cell and spatial omics data analysis

  2. General Statistical Methodologies: High-dimensional model inference and variable selection

  3. Statistics in Education: Statistical method for promoting education equity (reported by Forbes)
